Genrefication Resources

Genrefication is the process of arranging your library's fiction (and sometimes nonfiction) section by genre, similar to a bookstore, rather than by the author's last name. The goal is to make it easier for students to find books they'll enjoy.

  • Follett: "Genrefying Your School Library Collection"
    Link: https://www.follettcontent.com/school-libraries/genrefying
    Focus: Follett is a major vendor for school libraries and offers a wealth of free resources on this topic. You'll find a "Genrefication Best Practices" PDF, checklists, and examples for elementary, middle, and high schools.

  • Madison's Library: "Step-By-Step Guide to Genrefying Non-Fiction"
    Link: https://madisonslibrary.com/2023/09/25/ramblings-step-by-step-guide-to-genrefying-non-fiction/
    Focus: A practical, step-by-step guide from a librarian's blog. It covers auditing your collection, weeding, deciding on categories, choosing labels, and creating signage. It's a great "how-to" resource.

  • South Dakota State Library: "SDSL School Library Genrefication Guide"
    Link: https://library.sd.gov/LIB/SLC/doc/Guide-SDSL-SLGenrefication.pdf
    Focus: This PDF is a fantastic introductory guide that explains the "why" and "how" of genrefication. It breaks down the process into four clear steps: identify your why, make a plan, do the work, and finish up.

  • Demco Ideas & Inspiration: "What School Librarians Have to Say About Genrefication"
    Link: https://ideas.demco.com/blog/what-school-librarians-say-about-genrefication/
    Focus: This article compiles feedback from librarians who have gone through the process. It highlights the benefits they've seen, such as increased student engagement, a boost in circulation, and easier reshelving.

  • Junior Library Guild: "Five Reasons Why Librarians Choose Genrefication for Their School Libraries"
    Link: https://www.juniorlibraryguild.com/blog/post/five-reasons-why-librarians-choose-genrefication-for-their-school-libraries
    Focus: If you need to make the case for genrefication to your administration or colleagues, this article clearly lays out the key benefits, including how it encourages literacy and helps reluctant readers, ESL learners, and students with special needs.
