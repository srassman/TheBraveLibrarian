GCMS Library Improvement Plan

  • Going Dewey less - Genrefy the Library based on genre instead of Dewey numbers and authors. All other school libraries in our district are this way and will help students find and use materials more efficiently.
  • Update/Manage collection - The collection is out of date and not relevant to students which leads to low circulation numbers.
  • Using funds to update the collection in phases:
    • Fall '25 = fiction purchase with funds
    • Spring '26 = funds used for non-fiction.
    • I updated graphic novels and some fiction/non-fiction with purchases from this spring of '25 using Scholastic dollars and making a Permabound purchase.
  • Creating a useful library website for students, staff and community member.
  • Create a monthly newsletter for students, families and staff for library updates and resources.

Improvement of Book Collection Process Plan

  1. Weed out any books that are in really bad condition.
  2. Decide genre groups being used to organize books.
  3. Contact Follett company and speak to representative about getting genre lists for current collection.
  4. Order necessary genre labeling supplies.
  5. Map out the library where genre sections will be placed.
  6. Gather and make signage for sections.
  7. Begin to relabel all books with correct genre labels using list from the Follett representative.
  8. Move the newly labeled books to their new genre section of the library.
