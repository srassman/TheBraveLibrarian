Book Recommendations

Ground Zero

Author: Alan Gratz

Genre: Historical Fiction

Grades: 4th-7th

Lexile: 690L

Summary: Ground Zero tells the story of two kids, separated by nearly two decades but united by the same long war. In 2001, nine-year-old Brandon is in New York City, visiting his dad at work on the 107th floor of the World Trade Center when a plane hits the tower. In 2019, Reshmina is a young girl living in a war-torn Afghan village. When she stumbles upon a wounded American soldier, she must decide whether to help him, knowing it could put her family in grave danger. Their stories are worlds apart, but a shocking connection brings them together.

The Crossover

Author: Kwame Alexander

Genre: Realistic Fiction / Sports

Grades: 5th-8th

Lexile: 750L

Summary: Josh and Jordan Bell are twin brothers, basketball phenoms, and best friends. With a former pro-baller for a dad, their lives are all about the game. But when Jordan gets a girlfriend for the first time, the unbreakable bond between the twins starts to crack. As Josh struggles with feeling left behind, he has to navigate the complex world of brotherhood, family, and growing up. It’s a story about how life, like basketball, doesn’t have a playbook.

The Hunger Games

Author: Suzanne Collins

Genre: Dystopian / Science Fiction

Grades: 7th-12th

Lexile: 810L

Summary: In the ruins of what was once North America lies the nation of Panem, a shining Capitol surrounded by twelve outlying districts. As a cruel reminder of a past rebellion, the Capitol forces each district to send one boy and one girl, called Tributes, to compete in the annual Hunger Games—a televised fight to the death where there can be only one survivor.

Percy Jackson and The Lightning Thief

Author: Rick Riordan

Genre: Fantasy

Grades: 4th-8th

Lexile: 740L

Summary: The gods of Olympus are on the brink of war. Someone has stolen Zeus's master lightning bolt, the most powerful weapon ever made. All signs point to one unlikely suspect: a twelve-year-old New Yorker named Percy Jackson, who has just discovered he's not entirely human. Now, Percy must race against time to find the true thief and clear his name.
